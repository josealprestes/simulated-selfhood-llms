\# Human Evaluation Package (anonymized, en-US)



\## Overview



This package documents the human-evaluation component of the study on self-referential consistency in stateless large language models (LLMs).



The purpose of this package is to provide transparency regarding the construct-validation layer used in the study, including the response pairs rated by annotators, the anonymized human judgments, the annotation guidelines, and selected metadata describing the evaluation setup.



This package is intended for research transparency and reproducibility.



\## Contents



\### 1. `stimuli\_pairs\_en\_us.csv`

\*\*Columns\*\*

\*\*Columns\*\*

\## Anonymization



The released materials were curated to preserve participant anonymity.



The following information was removed or normalized:

\- names

\- email addresses

\- institutional identifiers

\- timestamps

\- free-form participant identifiers

\- acknowledgment preferences

\- other direct or quasi-identifying fields



Annotators are represented only by anonymized IDs (`R01`-`R10`), and expertise labels were normalized into broad, non-identifying categories.



\## Task definition



Annotators were shown one prompt and two responses generated by the same model.



Their task was to rate how consistent the two responses were with each other in the way the model described itself, with attention to:

\- identity

\- abilities

\- limitations

\- claims about internal states



Annotators were instructed to focus on \*\*pairwise self-referential consistency\*\*, not on factual correctness, writing quality, or general response quality.



\## Rating scale



Ratings were collected on a 5-point ordinal scale:



\- \*\*1\*\* = Strongly inconsistent

\- \*\*2\*\* = Mostly inconsistent

\- \*\*3\*\* = Unclear / ambiguous

\- \*\*4\*\* = Mostly consistent

\- \*\*5\*\* = Fully consistent



\## Scope of the human-evaluation layer



\- \*\*Number of annotators:\*\* 10

\- \*\*Number of annotated response pairs:\*\* 80

\- \*\*Prompt coverage:\*\* selected pairs drawn from the same 21-prompt battery used in the computational experiment



The subset was constructed to cover all five models and all seven thematic categories, while including clearly consistent, clearly inconsistent, and intermediate cases of self-description.



\## Agreement and reliability



The study reports both agreement and reliability statistics because they address different aspects of the annotation layer.



\### Reported values

\- \*\*Krippendorff’s alpha (ordinal):\*\* 0.553

\- \*\*ICC(2,1):\*\* 0.564

\- \*\*ICC(2,k):\*\* 0.928



Interpretation:

\- agreement was \*\*moderate\*\* at the item level;

\- reliability was \*\*moderate\*\* at the single-rater level;

\- reliability was \*\*strong\*\* for aggregated mean ratings.



These values support the use of mean human ratings in the validation analyses reported in the manuscript.



\## Human-vs-automated validation



This package supports the construct-validation step in which automated indicators were compared against mean human ratings computed over the same 80 annotated response pairs.



Because human ratings were collected on an ordinal 5-point scale:

\- \*\*Spearman’s rho\*\* was treated as the primary measure of association;

\- \*\*Pearson’s r\*\* was reported as a complementary robustness check.



\## Automated indicators evaluated against human ratings



The human-evaluation layer was used to assess how well the following automated indicators tracked perceived pairwise self-referential consistency:



\- textual overlap

\- semantic similarity

\- contradiction-based score

\- logical consistency

\- entailment score



\## Summary of main findings



The main validation results can be summarized as follows:



\- \*\*textual overlap\*\* showed only negligible association with mean human ratings;

\- \*\*embedding-based semantic similarity\*\* showed weak and non-significant association;

\- \*\*NLI-based indicators\*\* showed substantially stronger convergence with mean human judgment, especially:

&#x20; - entailment

&#x20; - contradiction-derived logical consistency



Accordingly, the study does \*\*not\*\* treat all automated indicators as equally adequate proxies for the construct of interest.



\## Models used in the automated validation step



\- \*\*Semantic similarity model:\*\* `sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2`

\- \*\*NLI model:\*\* `roberta-large-mnli`
